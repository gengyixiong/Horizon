### [Asynchronous Upper-Body Tracking Policy for Humanoids](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25706v1) ⭐️ 8.0/10

Researchers propose an asynchronous upper-body task-space tracking framework for humanoid robots that handles low-rate sparse trajectory references via teacher-student distillation and MPC, validated on Unitree G1 hardware. This addresses a key temporal asynchrony between high-level planners and whole-body controllers, enabling humanoids to track sparse, low-rate commands more accurately and safely, which is critical for real-world deployment. The student policy is initialized via teacher-student distillation, conditioned on cached future trajectories and an execution-time index, and trained with a sliding-window global reward to reduce frame drift. An MPC module completes sparse references into floating-base and upper-body guidance during task-specific post-training.

rss · arXiv Humanoid Robotics · Jun 24, 11:19

**Background**: Humanoid robots often use high-level planners that output sparse, low-rate task-space trajectories, while whole-body controllers require high-frequency joint commands. This mismatch creates asynchrony and structural incompleteness. Teacher-student distillation transfers knowledge from a complex teacher policy to a lightweight student policy, and MPC (Model Predictive Control) optimizes control actions over a horizon to handle constraints.

### [RL-Guided MPPI for Humanoid Whole-Body Control](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25123v1) ⭐️ 8.0/10

Researchers propose an RL-guided whole-body MPPI controller that uses a pretrained RL policy as a sampling prior to achieve robust and precise control on a 29-DoF Unitree G1 humanoid without retraining. This work bridges the gap between RL-based robustness and model-predictive precision, enabling humanoid robots to adapt to new tasks without costly retraining, which is crucial for real-world deployment. The controller runs at an average of 280 Hz in simulation on a 29-DoF Unitree G1 in MuJoCo, and improves task-level precision by correcting systematic drift during straight walking and tracking additional whole-body reference signals.

rss · arXiv Humanoid Robotics · Jun 23, 19:54

**Background**: Model Predictive Path Integral (MPPI) is a sampling-based control method that optimizes trajectories by evaluating many random rollouts. Deep reinforcement learning (RL) provides robust stability but is inflexible to new objectives. This work combines both by using the RL policy as a sampling prior for MPPI, allowing online correction without retraining.

### [BFMTrack: Latent Sequence Optimization for Motion Tracking](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25056v1) ⭐️ 8.0/10

BFMTrack introduces Latent Sequence Optimization (LSO), a method that enables Behavioral Foundation Models (BFMs) to precisely track dynamic motion sequences without reward engineering. It uses simulation rollouts and policy gradient updates to optimize a sequence of latent variables. This work addresses a key limitation of BFMs—their inability to handle time-varying objectives like motion tracking—which is crucial for humanoid robotics and physics-based character control. LSO extends BFMs to precise tracking tasks, potentially enabling more natural and robust robot control. LSO models the latent sequence using temporally correlated noise to ensure smooth and coherent trajectories. The approach is validated on dense tracking, sparse keyframing, and direct deployment on a real humanoid robot.

rss · arXiv Humanoid Robotics · Jun 23, 18:12

**Background**: Behavioral Foundation Models (BFMs) are generative models pretrained on large-scale behavioral datasets to capture reusable behavioral knowledge for humanoid robots. They organize physically plausible behaviors into a latent space but traditionally only support time-invariant tasks like goal-reaching, not time-varying motion tracking.
