Researchers introduced RoboTacDex, a large-scale multi-modal dataset of 6,000 dexterous manipulation trajectories on the Unitree G1 humanoid robot, covering 19 tasks, 23 skills, and 22 objects with visual, tactile, and action data. **Background**: Dexterous manipulation with humanoid robots requires rich sensory data (visual, tactile, action) to learn complex skills. Existing datasets often lack tactile feedback or focus on single-arm tasks. RoboTacDex fills this gap by providing comprehensive multi-modal data on a commercially available humanoid platform (Unitree G1), supporting research in imitation learning and robot skill acquisition.

### [RL Enables Humanoid Robot to Skate Dynamically](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.31807v1) ⭐️ 8.0/10

Researchers trained a reinforcement learning policy that enables a humanoid robot on passive inline skates to perform dynamic, edge-driven propulsion without human motion data or imitation learning. This work demonstrates a novel locomotion mode for humanoid robots that is more energy-efficient than walking, with up to 50% reduction in Cost of Transport, and could lead to faster, more agile humanoid robots for real-world applications. The policy was trained using different geometric wheel models (spherical and ellipsoidal) to overcome simulation contact artifacts, and it transferred zero-shot to the physical Booster T1 robot, demonstrating dynamic balance and perturbation rejection.

rss · arXiv Humanoid Robotics · Jun 30, 15:25

**Background**: Underactuated robotics deals with systems that have fewer actuators than degrees of freedom, making control challenging. Passive inline skates are underactuated because the wheels roll freely without direct drive. Reinforcement learning offers a way to discover control strategies for such complex dynamics without explicit programming.

### [ReactiveBFM: Real-Time Closed-Loop Whole-Body Control for Humanoids](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.30362v1) ⭐️ 8.0/10

ReactiveBFM introduces a closed-loop planning-control framework that enables humanoid robots to reactively coordinate whole-body motions in real-time by mitigating exposure bias and latency issues. This work addresses a critical limitation of current behavior foundation models, which only execute pre-defined motions and cannot react to environmental changes. ReactiveBFM enables zero-shot moving target reaching and achieves a 93.1% success rate under severe perturbations, significantly outperforming open-loop baselines. The framework uses a scheduled prefix sampling curriculum to train the generative planner to recover from imperfect states, and an asynchronous replanning mechanism with trajectory chunking to handle latency mismatch. It was deployed on the Unitree G1 humanoid and demonstrated text-conditioned closed-loop motions.

rss · arXiv Humanoid Robotics · Jun 29, 14:27

**Background**: Behavior Foundation Models (BFMs) are generative models pretrained on large-scale behavioral datasets to provide robust control priors for humanoid robots. However, they are trained with teacher forcing, which causes cumulative exposure bias when the model encounters imperfect states during closed-loop execution. ReactiveBFM solves this by training the planner on imperfect prefixes and using asynchronous replanning to match high-frequency tracking.
