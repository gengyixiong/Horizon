### [HumanoidUMI: Robot-Free Data Collection for Humanoid Whole-Body Manipulation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27239v1) ⭐️ 8.0/10

HumanoidUMI introduces a portable, robot-free framework that uses VR devices and UMI-inspired grippers to collect demonstration data for training humanoid whole-body manipulation policies. The system was validated in five real-world scenarios, demonstrating effective transfer of skills. This work addresses a key bottleneck in humanoid robot skill learning by eliminating the need for expensive robot teleoperation setups, making data collection more accessible and scalable. It enables whole-body behaviors that require coordinated perception, locomotion, and manipulation, which are critical for real-world humanoid deployment. HumanoidUMI collects sparse human keypoint trajectories, wrist-view observations, and gripper actions using lightweight VR devices and UMI-inspired grippers. The high-level policy predicts future keypoints, which are then retargeted to robot-native whole-body references and executed by a whole-body controller.

rss · arXiv Humanoid Robotics · Jun 25, 16:23

**Background**: The Universal Manipulation Interface (UMI) is a data collection and policy learning framework that uses hand-held grippers to capture human demonstrations for robot manipulation. Humanoid whole-body control requires coordinated motion of the entire robot body, including legs and arms, to perform tasks like grasping while walking. Traditional methods rely on robot teleoperation, which is hardware-intensive and limits scalability.

### [PressMimic: Pressure-Guided Humanoid Motion Imitation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26741v1) ⭐️ 8.0/10

PressMimic introduces a pressure-guided framework that integrates pressure sensing into both perception and control stages for humanoid motion imitation, using FRAPPE++ for multimodal pose estimation and a pressure-supervised policy (PSP) for reinforcement learning. This work addresses a critical gap in humanoid imitation by incorporating contact dynamics, reducing artifacts like foot sliding and floor penetration, and improving physical consistency, which is essential for real-world deployment of humanoid robots. The framework includes MotionPRO, a large-scale dataset with synchronized RGB, pressure, and motion capture data. Experiments show that pressure improves motion estimation accuracy, trajectory consistency, and execution stability.

rss · arXiv Humanoid Robotics · Jun 25, 08:24

**Background**: Humanoid motion imitation typically relies on vision-based motion capture and kinematic imitation, which ignore contact dynamics, leading to artifacts like foot sliding. Pressure sensing provides explicit contact and support constraints, helping to resolve ambiguity in vision-based estimation and enabling physically consistent contact patterns during execution.
