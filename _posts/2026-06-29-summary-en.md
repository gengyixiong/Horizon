Researchers introduced OpenHLM, a whole-body native vision-language-action (VLA) model that maps language instructions and camera images directly to all degrees of freedom of a humanoid robot, enabling coordinated locomotion and manipulation. This work addresses a key limitation of current humanoid robots, which often treat locomotion and manipulation separately, by enabling whole-body coordination. It paves the way for more capable and versatile humanoid robots that can perform complex tasks in real-world environments. OpenHLM is an empirical recipe that includes data collection, model architecture, and training procedures. It outputs joint-level actions for the entire body, allowing the robot to perform tasks like stepping on a pedal while manipulating an object.

rss · 人形机器人与具身智能（中文） · Jun 29, 01:07

**Background**: Traditional humanoid robots often use separate controllers for walking and arm manipulation, limiting their ability to coordinate whole-body movements. Vision-language-action (VLA) models combine visual perception, language understanding, and action generation, enabling robots to follow natural language commands. OpenHLM extends this concept to whole-body control, treating the entire robot as a single system.
