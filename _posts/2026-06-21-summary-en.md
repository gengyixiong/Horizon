### [First Universal Humanoid Robot Cerebellum Released](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMiSEFVX3lxTFBVRjFBYTVVMHhvUWtqdmxiZmZoaDRUcWg2YkVJTG1CT3BiM0ZENzdrUGhCdjNLTXVQRnBLNzZmclVkaF9RdTZQdA?oc=5) ⭐️ 8.0/10

The first universal 'cerebellum' for humanoid robots, named AstraBrain-WBC 0.5, has been released by Galaxy General, trained on 20,000 hours of human motion data to achieve zero-shot generalization. This breakthrough significantly advances humanoid robotics by enabling robots to generalize motion skills to unseen scenarios without additional training, potentially accelerating deployment in real-world applications like manufacturing and healthcare. The model is trained on the largest human motion dataset of its kind, comprising 20,000 hours of diverse activities. It demonstrates zero-shot generalization, meaning it can perform new tasks without fine-tuning.

**Background**: In robotics, the 'cerebellum' refers to the control system responsible for fine motor coordination and balance, analogous to the human cerebellum.
