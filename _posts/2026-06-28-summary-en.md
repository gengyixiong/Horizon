### [OpenHLM Enables Whole-Body Control for Humanoid Robots](https://news.google.com/rss/articles/CBMilAJBVV95cUxOVElKUkdVU0otNE5RVlRsakdpcmdST3drRWw5cTJwV2tTSWV3TVhtYUJETXpyUUh6OUFvQ05QdHVuRHQtN3Z3R0RONUVTNS0xaGlya0xiRlpNLWZESkxwZzU1YlhwYThRanY5dklWMVNza3FBN3ktNjkzX3VtcE9tRG1IYktmLVQ0cE1NUFZISUo2Wk5oS2ROQXhNVUxDMU1qR1Vsa045Ni01NlBWdnpobUxLVHZINnNydmwtaEFmdGdib3pVcERHeUxJR1ZoVkFabzNTeklsVFQtQUZJbGFCeUJFakFQNU5OaU5IZmx3bXNKS2ZTMlFISktkUmhXaF9YTXdmWnR4dGVBTEg5dXF3RXlhSHo?oc=5) ⭐️ 7.0/10

Researchers introduced OpenHLM, a system that enables humanoid robots to perform whole-body mobile manipulation, moving beyond simple bipedal platforms with arms. It uses a unified Vision-Language-Action (VLA) framework to coordinate locomotion and manipulation simultaneously. This breakthrough addresses a key limitation of humanoid robots, which previously could only walk or manipulate objects separately, not together. It paves the way for more capable and versatile humanoid robots that can perform complex real-world tasks like climbing stairs while carrying objects. OpenHLM is built on a latent action model (LAM) that learns from action-free egocentric videos, enabling the robot to infer actions without explicit demonstration. The system is part of the WholebodyVLA project, which is accepted at ICLR 2026.

rss · 人形机器人与具身智能（中文） · Jun 28, 02:01

**Background**: Traditional humanoid robots often function as 'walking arm platforms' where locomotion and manipulation are controlled separately, limiting their ability to perform coordinated whole-body tasks. Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models combine visual perception, language understanding, and action generation to enable more natural robot control. OpenHLM extends this concept to whole-body loco-manipulation.
