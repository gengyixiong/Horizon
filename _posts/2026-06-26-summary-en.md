### [HumanoidUMI: Robot-Free Data Collection for Humanoid Whole-Body Manipulation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27239v1) ⭐️ 8.0/10

HumanoidUMI introduces a portable, robot-free framework that uses VR devices and UMI-inspired grippers to collect whole-body demonstration data for humanoid robots, enabling transferable skill learning without requiring a robot during data collection. This framework addresses a key bottleneck in humanoid robot skill learning by making high-quality whole-body demonstration data accessible without expensive robot hardware or expert teleoperation, potentially accelerating the development of general-purpose humanoid robots. The system collects sparse human keypoint trajectories, wrist-view observations, and gripper actions, which are used to train a high-level policy that predicts future keypoints; these are then retargeted to robot-native whole-body references and executed by a whole-body controller.

rss · arXiv Humanoid Robotics · Jun 25, 16:23

**Background**: Humanoid robots require whole-body coordination for tasks like manipulation while walking, but collecting demonstration data typically requires teleoperating the robot itself, which is costly and inefficient. The Universal Manipulation Interface (UMI) previously demonstrated a robot-free approach for fixed-base manipulators. HumanoidUMI extends this concept to whole-body humanoid control by using VR devices to capture human motion and grippers for end-effector actions.

### [PressMimic: Pressure-Guided Motion Capture and Control for Humanoid Imitation](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.26741v1) ⭐️ 8.0/10

PressMimic introduces a framework that integrates pressure sensing into both motion capture and control for humanoid robots, using a multimodal model (FRAPPE++) and a pressure-supervised policy (PSP) to improve physical grounding and reduce artifacts like foot sliding. This work addresses a critical gap in humanoid motion imitation by incorporating contact dynamics, leading to more stable and physically consistent robot behaviors, which is essential for real-world deployment. The framework includes a new large-scale dataset (MotionPRO) with synchronized RGB, pressure, and motion capture data. Experiments show that pressure improves motion estimation accuracy, trajectory consistency, and execution stability.

rss · arXiv Humanoid Robotics · Jun 25, 08:24

**Background**: Humanoid motion imitation typically relies on vision-based motion capture and kinematic imitation, ignoring contact dynamics, which leads to artifacts like foot sliding and floor penetration. Pressure sensing provides explicit contact and support constraints that help resolve ambiguity in vision-based estimation and enable physically consistent contact patterns during execution.

### [Asynchronous Upper-Body Task-Space Tracking for Humanoids](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.25706v1) ⭐️ 8.0/10

This paper proposes an asynchronous upper-body task-space tracking framework for humanoid robots that handles temporal asynchrony between sparse high-level plans and high-frequency whole-body control, validated on Unitree G1 hardware. This work addresses a critical gap in humanoid control by enabling robust tracking despite low-rate, sparse reference trajectories, which is essential for deploying humanoids in real-world tasks where high-level planners operate at slower rates. The framework uses teacher-student distillation to initialize a student policy conditioned on a cached future trajectory and an execution-time index, and employs an MPC module for task-specific post-training to complete sparse references and constrain policy drift.

rss · arXiv Humanoid Robotics · Jun 24, 11:19

**Background**: Humanoid robots often have high-level planners that output sparse, low-rate task-space trajectories, while low-level whole-body controllers must run at high frequency for stability. This temporal asynchrony creates a challenge for tracking. Teacher-student distillation is a technique where a smaller student model learns from a larger teacher model, often used to compress policies for efficient deployment. Model Predictive Control (MPC) is a control method that optimizes future actions over a horizon, commonly used for whole-body control of humanoids.
